# White Paper: Institutional Structural Framework (v3.1)

## 1. The Core Philosophy
The system operates on **Structural Invalidation**. Every trade is a thesis: "If price is here, then the institutional order at level X must protect the zone." The moment price moves beyond that structural point, the thesis is dead, and we exit.

---

## 2. Threshold Justifications (First Principles)
Every threshold in this system is derived from market mechanics:
*   **ToD RVOL > 2.0**: Double the historical participation for this exact time slot indicates deliberate institutional activity, not random retail flow.
*   **Zone Score > 60**: Represents the "Grade A" minimum: At least 2 clean bounces + above-median volume + above-median move efficiency.
*   **HTF Alignment**: We trade only when the "Big Money" (Daily) and "Medium Money" (1H) have both committed to a directional structure.

---

## 3. Dimension 1: Context (Daily & 1H Structure)
**The Logic**: Fractal Structural Alignment.
*   **Daily Bias**: Bullish only if Daily timeframe shows 2 consecutive Higher Lows + Higher Highs. Bearish only if 2 consecutive Lower Highs + Lower Lows.
*   **1H Structure**: Must align with the Daily Bias using HH/HL or LH/LL.
*   **Assumption**: Structure is a "Lead" indicator of commitment; EMAs are "Lag" indicators of average price. We trade commitment.

---

## 4. Dimension 2: Location (Zone Quality & Invalidation)
*   **Invalidation Rule**: A zone is considered "Dead" if price achieves two consecutive body closes through the level on above-average volume.
*   **Logic**: A broken zone means the institutional "Iceberg" order has been fully filled or abandoned.

---

## 5. Dimension 3: Execution (Structural Stops)
We abandon ATR-based stops. The stop goes where the **thesis is proven wrong**:
*   **Strategy A (Sweep Reversal)**: Stop is **1 tick beyond the sweep wick**.
    *   *Thesis*: If price returns below the sweep low, the "Institutional Trap" has failed.
*   **Strategy B (Breakout Acceptance)**: Stop is **0.3 * ATR below the broken structure level**.
    *   *Thesis*: If price re-enters the old range, the "Acceptance" was a fakeout.
*   **Minimum Guard**: 0.5 * ATR (Prevents stops from being tighter than the natural spread/noise of the instrument).

---

## 6. Dimension 4: Trade Management (Dynamic Exits)
*   **Target Profit (TP)**: The TP is the **next valid Grade A zone** in the signal direction.
*   **Minimum RR**: Trade is only taken if the distance to the next zone is at least **1.5x the risk**.
*   **Partial Profit**: 50% of the position is closed at **1.5R profit**.
*   **Break-Even**: Upon hitting TP1 (1.5R), the stop is moved to the **Entry Price** to guarantee a "Risk-Free" trade.

---

## 7. Validation: Rolling Walk-Forward
*   **Methodology**: Instead of a single 70/30 split, we use **Rolling 3-Month Windows**.
*   **Stability**: The system must show positive expectancy across at least 4 out of 6 independent rolling windows to be considered robust.
